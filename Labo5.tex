\section{Too few items in a table row}

what happens when we have too few items in a row:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Animal & Food & Size \\
\midrule
dog & meat \\  
horse & hay & large \\
frog \\  
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\textbf{Observation:} Empty cells are created for missing items. The table still compiles but may look incomplete.

What happens when we have too many items:

\textbf{Observation:} LaTeX throws an error: \texttt{Extra alignment tab has been changed to \textbackslash cr}. The compilation fails until the extra items are removed.

\subsection{Table with notes}
Using \texttt{threeparttable} for footnotes:
\begin{center}
\begin{threeparttable}
\caption{Animal Characteristics with Notes}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Animal & Diet & Lifespan \\
\midrule
Dog & Omnivorous & 10-13 years\tnote{1} \\
Cat & Carnivorous & 12-15 years \\
Tortoise & Herbivorous & 80-150 years\tnote{2} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\begin{tablenotes}
\item [1] Varies by breed and size
\item [2] Some species can live even longer
\end{tablenotes}
\end{threeparttable}
\end{center}
